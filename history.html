    • Brewery's Beginning- 1786

    At age 18, John Molsonn sailed on a leaking ship from England to Canada, with a thirst for a better beer in a new country. In 1786, he founded the Molson Brewery, the oldest brewery in North America, and subsequently, Canada's second oldest company.

    Railroad to Success- 1836

    In the spirit of bringing people together, John Molson helped creat the first ever railway connecting Canadians to our neighbours in the south. The Champlain and St Lawrence Railroad made the journey from Montreal to New York much faster.

    Export's Entry- 1903

    Molson Export entered the Canadian market in 1903 as a refreshing, well-balanced golden ale. With ingredients dating back to the the foundation of the Molson Brewery, Export has a history as rich as its taste.

    Counting on Molson- 1950

    In 1950, Molson installed Quebec's largest functioning clock on their side of the montreal factory. Visible from 12km away, the Molson clock is a staple of the Montreal skyline, counting great moments in the city's history for 68 years and counting. Now, the iconic hexagon shape of the clock has been integrated into the new Molson branding as a celebration of our heritage.

    Molson Hospital- 1821

    When Molson saw that the only hospital in Montreal had a mere 30 beds, he took it upon himself to lobby support to build the first public hospital in the city. By May of that same year, the Montreal General Hospital was opened.

    Banking on great Endeavors- 1855

    The Molson family's contributions to Canadian banking begain with John Molson acquiring an American bank and transforming it into the fully Canadian-owned Montreal Bank. In 1855, brothers William and John Molson, Jr. founded Molsons Bank to help a growing economy flourish. In subsequent years, younger generations of the Molson family would lead Molsons Bank, continuing the legacy of this bold venture into finance.

    Returning to Sustainability- 1927

    In 1927, Molson helped establish the first ever bottle-return program. It was an early step in our commitment towards sustainability and waste reduction that continues to this day.

    An icon is born- 1959

    Molson Canadian came to market in 1959, supported with a TV campaign that asked "What do you want from a lager?", and a fresh-tasting beer that proved we had listened. It was the arrival of an iconic mainstay in the Canadian beer market.
