    • Centuries ago, two entrepreneurs-to-be each began their separate journey to success.

    Brothers' Beginning- 1786

    In 1786 an Englishman named John Molson founded the Molson Brewery after making the move to Canada. His business would go on to be a major success and source of Canadian Pride

    Railroad to Success- 1836

    Almost a full century later and just south of the border, another Englishman named Augustus Chesebrough took up leave from Europe to come to North America, this time settling in Pennsylvania. After doing research around the petroleum discovered in the area he purified the excess and produced what would eventually become Vaseline, a restorative jelly for damaged skin. Both businessmen continued to develop successful brands that have come to be household names hundreds of years later

    Export's Entry- 1903

    But what they didn’t count on was that the fate of their companies would eventually become parallel. On February 14, 2020 every single person affiliated at the executive level of each company suddenly and mysteriously disappeared, all replaced the same day by hundreds of individuals, all without any sort of history or proof of existing prior to that day. This new company was spearheaded by a somewhat familiar name, but uncannily altered. Mr August Cheesebrow is now found to be at the head of a new company, a hastily spliced combination of the previously independent companies.

    Counting on Molson- 1950

    With the unveiling of this odd new company many consumers of the old products questioned the sudden drastic changes. Shortly after a public outcry began over the loss of the previous companies the CEO Cheesebrow came out to the public with the reasoning for the merge. As it turns out, the suspicious activity traces all the way back to the 18th and 19th century, in Europe. As it turns out, the parents of Molson and Chesebrough had already decided to do a little ‘content mixing’ of their own and thus August Cheesebrow, initially born under the name Cheegus Molson-Chesebrough was conceived.

    Molson Hospital- 1821

    Born as a terrible adulterous secret the boy was hidden away from the public for decades. Feeling pity the parents and a small group of supportive close friends kept watch over the young boy. After spending so many years hidden from the sunlight, his body developed a unique sensitivity to sunlight wherein he would dry up and burn to exposure. When one day his parents arrived with the products of the elder childrens’ companies, Cheegus (who is a natural scientist similar to his non-bastard older brothers) discovered a careful solution of both Molson lager and a variant of Vaseline called Intensive Care- Cocoa Radiant will keep his body protected from the harsh sun

    Banking on great Endeavors- 1855

    As his parents’ friends spent so much time looking after him they had also developed a sensitivity to light. To their amazement, Cheegus showed them the amazing healing possibilities of the new mixture. Years went by with Cheegus making secret outings to the surface with his protective jelly-lager (eventually shortened to jellager). He spent the entirety of his time researching business law. As he grew older he discovered that his solution not only shielded him from the sun, it also protected his body from major symptoms of aging!

    Returning to Sustainability- 1927

    Upon realizing the potential of his new product he used the knowledge he collected on the surface to expertly seize claim to both the Molson and Vaseline companies. In a single day he quickly disposed of all the previous employees (especially the offspring of his half siblings) and brought in his now ancient following of jelly-lagered up people to fill in the vacancies. He changes his name to appear eerily similar to his late half-brother to be officially known as Cheegus Cheesebrow. He confided in the consumers of the previous products that his new product would make them completely forget about what they used to purchase in favour of his Jellager. Doubt was quickly erased from the consumers’ minds as they discovered the restorative qualities of Cheesebrow’s product. The history of Molson and Vaseline quickly eroded from people’s minds as they relished in the anti-aging jelly-lager that kept their bodies looking young (or at least, the age they were when they began using it).

    An icon is born- 1959

    Just as the two companies blew up in success from a quality product so too did Cheesebrow’s solution. In a rather sick twist Cheesebrow rebranded his personal discovery to either honour or ridicule its history with becoming one from two and dubbed his company Molson. Not wanting to expend any finances on branding to keep focus on production he asked the remaining Vaseline designers to utilize the Molson name and Vaseline branding to produce new branding for him.
